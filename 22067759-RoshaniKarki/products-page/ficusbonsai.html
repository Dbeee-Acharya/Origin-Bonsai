Plant Details

The Ficus is a popular choice for bonsai enthusiasts due to its easy care and ability to withstand a wide range of temperatures. There are many different types of Ficus that can be grown as bonsai, including the Ficus microcarpa (also known as the Chinese Banyan) and the Ficus retusa (also known as the Indian Laurel). Here are a few tips for caring for your Ficus bonsai: Provide your bonsai with plenty of indirect sunlight, as it needs at least 4-6 hours of sunlight per day to thrive. Water your bonsai regularly, being sure to keep the soil evenly moist but not waterlogged. Fertilize your bonsai tree with a balanced bonsai fertilize every month during the growing season. Prune your bonsai regularly to maintain its desired shape and encourage new growth. With proper care, your Ficus bonsai should thrive and bring a touch of natural beauty to your home or garden.
