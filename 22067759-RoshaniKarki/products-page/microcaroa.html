Plant Details

Ficus microcarpa, also known as the Chinese banyan or Indian laurel, is a species of fig tree native to Southeast Asia and India. It is a popular choice for bonsai cultivation due to its compact size, attractive foliage, and ability to withstand pruning and training. The leaves of Ficus microcarpa are large and glossy, and the tree produces small, green fruit that are attractive to wildlife. It is a relatively fast-growing species that can tolerate a wide range of conditions, but it prefers well-draining soil and a sunny location. In its native habitat, Ficus microcarpa can grow into a large tree, but it can be kept small and maintained as a bonsai with regular pruning and training.
